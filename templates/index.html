Overview

An exoplanet or extrasolar planet is a planet outside the Solar System. The first possible evidence of an exoplanet was noted in 1917 but was not then recognized as such. The first confirmation of the detection occurred in 1992. A different planet, first detected in 1988, was confirmed in 2003.
According to statistics from the NASA Exoplanet Archive, As of 4 October 2024, there are 5,765 confirmed exoplanets in 4,304 planetary systems, with 965 systems having more than one planet.
The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is expected to discover more exoplanets, and to give more insight into their traits, such as their composition, environmental conditions, and potential for life
